Twippe App

Twippe is a project created by Fabian Perez at Nuclio Digital School during the master in Digital Product Management in Barcelona.

This project was born from my passion about cycling and technology and also a pain that I was suffering as an amateur cyclist (user) while living in Barcelona.

Starts with describing a context of the sitation as well as the opportunity that there is in the current market, also validated it from a big competitor that you may know if you like sports: Strava.

Context

Mission, vision and values are the pilars of this project and you can find them wherever you in this travel, the values are: Community, Balance, Creativity, Closeness, Passion. You can check more information about it in Twippe's website.

Our mission is to create a community of cycling making it more interactive, fun and challenging through technology.

Once we defined our integrity as a product, we established our market target (for sure in Barcelona - we can set a call and I will give the reason). A Value Proposition Canvas was created with its "customer jobs", "pains", "gains", etc. For the Benchmark we use the main competitors, some of them are: Strava, Runtastic, Koomot and others, The research was about their business model, payments, monetization, marketing, which kind of maps they use, amount of users, ratings in the stores, etc.

User persona

At the beginning only defined a single User Persona, but after a couple of iterations more (surveys, research, etc) we ended up with 3 different User Personas.

Product Strategy

In this part of the project a Business Canvas Model was created, also a Unique Selling Proposition that was refined several times meanwhile we learned more about our users and identify other possibles differentiations between the apps in the current market. Along with this we set our elevator pitch, do you want to hear it? Email me!

Metrics & KPIS

We decided to use North Star Framework for the metrics (Guess why!, let's discuss in a call). Hint: our main metric is Amount of users active per month and we really care about engament.

Our metrics were develop from questions like: How many users are engaging? What level of engagement are they reaching? How often does each user engage?

OKRs

We defined 3 different objectives for the first year. First 2 quarters really focus on validating and refinening the idea and business model. The last 2 quarters more on get as many users as possible. The 3 objectives also includes 3 key results and its activities to reach the goal.

MVP Pre-product

During stage of the product the idea was to validate all our assumptions and try to get some emails from possible users and testers for the first version. For this MVP Pre-product we created:

  • Logo
  • Website (https://twippe.app)
  • Prototype (using Figma)
  • Instagram profile (www.instagram.com/twippeapp)
  • Survey using Google Forms

Fail fast - learn fast

After running the Google Form survey for aobut 30 hours we received more than 300 answers and more than 100 emails from possible users, with a CPL of 0.08 euro. We obtained very useful data validating several of our hipotesis and also learning about others scenarios. We spent a total of 10 euros in an instagram campaing.

MVP

The first MVP is under construction with the integration of Garmin as the first device for cyclist. It is going to be only an APP project available for IOs and Android using React Native.

User Story Mapping

After validating the first assumptions we used User Story Mapping to understand the flow of the our users to achieve one specific goal and what could be the possible user stories. This also help us to select the first user storys that we think are more critical for the first version and which others can come in future releases.

Epics & Prioritization

After the epics (at high level) were created, we selected MOSCoW to prioritaze the possible stories based on the User Story Mapping and also the Benchmark told us what are the basic features we need to include in order to able to offer the minumum things the users are expecting and for sure our value proposition must be included at this point.

Marketing & Adquisition

Besides the justifying of our digital channels (the majority of the data here came from the surveys we did in early stages while validating apps, webs our possible users use most) to create our go to market campaing, we added a model of market prenetration using influencers and popular cyclists around Spain and then scaling the model to Europe and other continents.
